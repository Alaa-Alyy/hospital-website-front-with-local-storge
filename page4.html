The Truth About Cholesterol

By Dr. Karim Nassar, Cardiologist, Heart & Vascular Center

Cholesterol is a waxy substance found in your blood. While your body needs cholesterol to build healthy cells, having high levels can increase your risk of heart disease.

Types of Cholesterol

  • LDL (bad cholesterol): Can build up in your arteries and increase heart risk.
  • HDL (good cholesterol): Helps remove LDL from your bloodstream.

How to Manage Cholesterol

  • Eat more fiber and healthy fats, such as those found in nuts, seeds, and fish.
  • Exercise regularlyâ€”aim for at least 30 minutes most days.
  • Avoid trans fats and processed foods.
  • Get your cholesterol checked regularly, especially if you have a family history of heart disease.

Talk to your doctor about your cholesterol numbers and the best plan for your health. Small lifestyle changes can make a big difference!
