Understanding Mental Burnout: Signs & How to Recover

By Dr. Omar El Sherif

Mental burnout is a growing problem in our fast-paced world. It's more than just feeling tired—it's a state of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion that can affect your work, relationships, and overall well-being.

Common Signs of Burnout

  • Constant fatigue and lack of motivation
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Feeling detached, alone, or hopeless
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
  • Increased irritability or frustration

How to Recover

  • Take regular breaks and allow yourself to rest, even if it's just a few minutes each hour.
  • Talk to someone you trust about how you're feeling. You're not alone.
  • Practice relaxation techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or gentle exercise.
  • Set boundaries and learn to say no when you need to.
  • Seek professional help if you feel overwhelmed or unable to cope.

Remember, your mental health is just as important as your physical health. Taking care of yourself is not selfish—it's necessary.
